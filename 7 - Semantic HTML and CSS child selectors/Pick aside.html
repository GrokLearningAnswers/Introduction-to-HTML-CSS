Round 16 Women's World Cup: Brazil vs. Australia

When: Sunday, 1:00 p.m. EDT

Where: Mocton Stadium, Moncton, Canada

If they beat old foe, Brazil, it will be the third time in a row that the Matilda's have made it to the quarter finals of the Women's World Cup.

In 2007, when they reached the final eight, it was Brazil who thwarted their run with a 3-2 win. Brazil went on to be runners up against a better German side 2-0: the best that Brazil has achieved to date. In 2011, Brazil caused the Aussies some pain again with a narrow 1-0 loss in Group D. So you could say these teams have some World Cup history.

Can Brazil, led by the amazing Marta (like other Brazilian soccer players before her, no family name required!) best the Matildas in three consecutive World Cups? Marta has had a quiet Cup so far, but as five-time FIFA World Player of the Year and the tournament's all-time leading goal-scorer, she will always be dangerous.

Or will the Matildas, having grown in skill and experience from the foundations of the young 2011 World Cup team, begin to even the ledger?
