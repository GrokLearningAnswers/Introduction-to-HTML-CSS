Stunning Rainbow in Sydney!

Image credit: Nick Verne

The storm clouds parted and Sydney was greeted by a spectacular double rainbow. Social media exploded in response, as people rushed to share their photos online.

Professor Blaggins from the School of Physics at the University of Argleton said that "double rainbows are caused by a double reflection of sunlight inside the raindrops.

"The secondary rainbow is fainter than the primary because more light is absorbed from two reflections compared to one, and the secondary rainbow is spread over more of the sky".
